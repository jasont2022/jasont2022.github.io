How to Thrive in University: Common Questions and Answers or Advice to Help You Succeed in University

Q&A

1. What should I do when I am not getting the most of my classes?

The possible answer could be that you are not picking a major or set of classes that excites you the most. To get the most of your classes, pick classes that you are passionate in learning and you will feel the most achievable or happy with the results you end up with.

2. What should I do when I am struggling with this one class and I just want to get it over with it?

The first sugesstion for you is to decide whether this class is important to your success in terms of learning or career wise. This will determine if you can just drop or pass the class if it is not a requirement or something important to your studies. If this class is important to your studies like a mandatory requirement, it would not hurt to talk to the professor. The professor can help you with your studies, guide you through the class, and even provide useful advices on how to struggle less with the class. The professor is responsible for the content or materials for the class and they in the end determine your grades.

3. Why are my grades are not reflecting my best effort I put into my classes?

The grades from your classes consist of multiple aspects such as homework, projects, midterms, particaption, etc. Most likely you are receving low grades with one or more aspects in the class. The best advice is to realize where you are struggling with and to find ways to improve on that aspect. You can also discuss with your professors to see why your effort is not being reflected in your grade. For example, one might be extremely successful in homework, but is struggling with exams. This could be not studying for exams, spending less time doing practice exam questions, or not holding one self to truly understand the class materials.

4. What should I do when I feel completely lost or clueless about what I am learning in my classes?

The first suggestion for you is to talk to the professor of the classes you feel completely lost. They help you catch up with the speed of the class or even provide extensions on work if nessecary. The professor of the class can provide useful tips for you to succeed in thier classes. The second suggestion is to attend office hours of the professor or TAs as they can always help answer your questions in class materials or homework.

5. What should I do when the pace of my classes are fast and I am extremely behind?

Same as above, the suggestion would be for you to talk to the professor of the class as they determine the pace and materials of the class. They can provide advice in terms of which materials or assignments to priortize and provide extensions if needed.

6. How do I "make time" for myself to do anything besides studying all the time?

One suggestion would be to find some days of the week to recharge or have a longer period of downtime. On your downtime, do something that you enjoy besides academics and do not think about your work, enjoy the time you have spent on your break. This will prevent burnt out and to feel recharge. Everyone has the same 24 hours, no one has extra hours. So, be wise with the time you spend doing, in this case, enjoying your break!

7. Why do I feel unmotivated to continue my studies or even putting in effort into my classes?

There are many possibilities with this question. One possibility could be that you are burnt out for your classes. Another could be that you are not enojoying or learning as much from your classes. With these possibilities, the suggestion would be to reflect why you are feeling this way and to figure out your answer. Also, it would not hurt to seek out help and to discuss your feelings with ours. If your answer is to the disucssions above check it out! The most important take away from this, is that you are feeling passionate about what you are doing and that you are taking care of yourself.
